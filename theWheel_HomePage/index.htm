theWheel is a tool for personal knowledge management.  Actually, it can manage anything that can be represented as interconnected pieces of information.  This could be your CD collection, or pictures from your last vacation, or your favorite places on the Internet, or your favorite ice cream recipes, or nearly anything else that you can think of.

 

theWheel manages pieces of information, or nodes, by tracking the links among the nodes.  The link between any two nodes is assigned a weight, so that two nodes (two CDs, two books, two ice cream recipes) that are closely related have a high link weight.  If two nodes are not at all related, they have a link weight of zero.

 

Using theWheel is easy.  A group of nodes that are interconnected form a space.  One of the nodes in the space is designated as the entry point into the space.  When the space is first opened in theWheel, this node is displayed prominently near the center of the main window into the space (the space view).  Other closely related nodes are displayed around it.  Moving through the space is simple and intuitive:  point at a node that you are interested in.  As you do, that node and related nodes become more prominent.  Watch a screen capture to see how easy it is to use, and then go and download here.
